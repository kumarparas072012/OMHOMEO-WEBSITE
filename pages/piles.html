Understanding Piles (Hemorrhoids)

A quick patient guide to symptoms, supportive homeopathic care, and safety red flags.

Overview

Piles are swollen veins in the lower rectum or around the anus. They can be internal or external and are commonly linked to constipation, prolonged straining, pregnancy, or long sitting hours.

Common Symptoms

  • Bright red bleeding during or after passing stool
  • Itching, irritation, swelling, or discomfort around the anus
  • Painful lump near the anus in thrombosed external piles

Homeopathic Clinical View

Homeopathic treatment is individualized according to bowel pattern, pain profile, digestion, and constitutional tendency. Care is combined with hydration, fibre intake, and bowel habit correction.

Persistent bleeding or severe pain still requires colorectal medical evaluation.

When to Seek Urgent Care

Seek immediate medical care for heavy bleeding, dizziness, black stools, fever, or severe persistent pain.
