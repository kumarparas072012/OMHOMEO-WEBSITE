Overview

An anal fissure is a small tear in the anal lining, often caused by hard stool, constipation, repeated straining, or local trauma. It can become chronic if untreated.

Common Symptoms

  • Sharp or burning pain during and after stool passage
  • Fresh blood on stool or toilet paper
  • Anal spasm, local irritation, or tenderness

Homeopathic Clinical View

Homeopathic case-taking considers stool consistency, pain character, healing pattern, and bowel habits. Supportive measures include hydration, fibre-rich diet, and warm sitz baths.

Long-standing fissures may require medical or procedural treatment.

When to Seek Medical Advice

Consult promptly if pain or bleeding continues beyond 1–2 weeks, or if fever, pus discharge, or marked swelling appears.
