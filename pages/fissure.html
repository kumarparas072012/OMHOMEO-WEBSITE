What is an Anal Fissure?

An anal fissure is a small tear in the lining of the anus, which can cause pain and bleeding during bowel movements.

Causes

  • Constipation and straining during bowel movements
  • Diarrhea
  • Childbirth
  • Anal intercourse
  • Inflammatory bowel disease

Symptoms

  • Sharp pain during bowel movements
  • Bleeding from the anus
  • Itching or irritation around the anus
  • A visible tear or cut in the skin

Treatment Options

Treatment for anal fissures may include:

  • Increased fiber intake and hydration
  • Topical anesthetics
  • Sitz baths
  • Medications to relax the anal sphincter
  • Surgery in severe cases
