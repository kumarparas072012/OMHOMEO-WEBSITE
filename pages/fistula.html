What is a Fistula?

A fistula is an abnormal connection between two body parts, often occurring in the anal region. It can result from an infection or an abscess.

Symptoms of Fistula

  • Pain and discomfort in the anal area
  • Swelling and redness
  • Discharge of pus or blood
  • Foul-smelling drainage

Treatment Options

Treatment for fistulas may include:

  • Antibiotics to treat infection
  • Surgery to close the fistula
  • Drainage of any abscesses
