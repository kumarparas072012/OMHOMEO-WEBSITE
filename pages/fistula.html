Understanding Anal Fistula

Patient-focused information on fistula symptoms, supportive care, and treatment urgency.

Overview

An anal fistula is an abnormal tunnel between the anal canal and skin around the anus, usually following an anal abscess or infection. It often needs specialist surgical assessment.

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent or recurrent discharge near the anus
  • Pain, swelling, and irritation around the anal region
  • Repeated abscess formation or fever episodes

Homeopathic Clinical View

Homeopathic medicines may be used as supportive care for pain, inflammation, and healing tendency after complete case evaluation. Lifestyle and bowel regulation are advised alongside.

Definitive fistula management generally requires colorectal specialist planning.

When to Seek Urgent Care

Urgent care is needed for fever, severe pain, increasing swelling, foul discharge, or inability to pass stool comfortably.
